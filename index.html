OptiWeight-PC Decision Aid

Introduction

This tool helps patients, clinicians and policy-makers evaluate weight-loss programmes for men with BMI 27–35 kg/m² awaiting robotic radical prostatectomy. It combines patient preference data (from a discrete-choice experiment) with cost-effectiveness parameters to predict uptake and net value.

Example benchmark: Semaglutide (Wegovy) reduces BMI by ≈5.5 kg/m² in 12 months but causes nausea in >30 % of patients; lifestyle programmes are safer but slower. Navigate the tabs, adjust inputs, compare scenarios and export a PDF report.
